Why Accessibility in Storyline
Starts with Slide Architecture

Most Storyline developers bolt accessibility on at the end — adding alt text just before publishing and hoping automated checkers don't catch anything serious. After building dozens of ADA and Section 508-compliant courses, one truth stands out: the moment you treat accessibility as a finishing step, you've already failed. It must be the first architectural decision — built into master slides, focus order, colour palette, and interaction design before a single piece of content is written.

Tab order, focus management, colour contrast, keyboard navigation — none of these can be reliably fixed after a course is built. This article walks through exactly how to structure Storyline templates to make accessibility the default, not the exception.
